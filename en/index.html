About Capoeira

Capoeira is an Afro-Brazilian art form that incorporates rhythmic music, dance, core-strengthening kicks, and flexibility-improving escapes into playful expression. Capoeira is a game. Learn to play with us!

For a better sense of Capoeira and what we do here, please visit our YouTube Channel. It features some of the video made by the teacher ("Professor Zumbi") and his group ("Cordão de Ouro Seoul").

Whenever you are ready for instruction, please inquire about taking classes. Classes are taught by Professor Zumbi and his senior students.

Classes

Classes are held six days a week, from Saturday to Thursday. On weekends we open from 12 noon to 3 PM. On weekdays, the studio is open from 4:30 PM to 10:30 PM.

The Capoeira Studio has classes for children (ages 7 - 12) and adults. Teenagers are welcome to join the classes for adults.

The academy opens at 4:30 PM on Tuesdays and Thursdays for the Capoeira class for kids. No prior experience with Capoeira is required for the kids class.

Weekdays

We cover the fundamentals of Capoeira (kicks, escapes, and moving on your hands) in our class for beginners on weekdays at 6:30 PM. No prior Capoeira experience or athletic ability is required in the class for beginners. After the first 50 minutes, beginners have the option to stay and play Capoeira with more senior students in a roda. Beginners are encouraged to participate in the roda to apply the material learned in class. The roda is free.

The class for advanced students starts with an optional hour long roda that includes Capoeira music and singing. To participate in the roda please arrive an hour before the official start of the advanced class. Starting at 8:30 PM we train advanced Capoeira sequences and acrobatics until around 10 PM.

You can find more information about Professor Zumbi and his teaching philosophy by visiting his website. You can discover more about our group by visiting our group ("CDO Seoul") website.
